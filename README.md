# Inventory-Risk-Analysis
This project builds a Supply Chain Risk Dashboard for DataCo Global to identify high-risk shipping routes, delayed order patterns, and low-margin categories — enabling the operations team to prioritise intervention and reduce the late delivery rate

## Business Problem
DataCo Global's operations team needed visibility into which shipping 
routes, regions, and modes were generating the highest delivery risk 
and margin loss — with no existing early-warning system in place.

## Tools
- MYSQL
- Python (Pandas, Numpy)
- Power BI 

## Key Findings
### Late Deliveries Are the Biggest Operational Risk

- 103K orders (57.3% of total orders) were classified as Late deliveries.
- Late deliveries significantly exceeded Early (43K) and On-Time (34K) shipments.
- This indicates substantial inefficiencies in logistics execution and fulfillment processes.

⭐ Business Impact: Higher customer dissatisfaction, increased operational costs, and potential revenue loss.

## Standard Class Shipping Carries the Highest Risk Exposure
- Standard Class accounted for approximately 108K high-risk orders (59.7% of total risk orders).
- First Class and Same Day shipments showed considerably lower risk concentrations.

⭐ Recommendation: Prioritize process improvements and carrier performance reviews for Standard Class shipments.

## Western Europe and Central America Generate the Highest Sales Volumes
- Western Europe contributed approximately 31.5% of total sales.
- Central America contributed approximately 30.3% of total sales.

⭐ Business Impact: Any delivery disruptions in these regions could have a disproportionate effect on revenue and customer retention.

## Late Orders Are Driving Significant Profit Leakage
- Late shipment orders generated approximately 2.23M in profit impact.
- High volumes of delayed orders suggest hidden costs from expedited shipping, returns, and customer service interventions.

⭐ Recommendation: Reduce late delivery rates in high-volume regions to improve profitability.

## Profit Margins Are Relatively Stable Across Shipment Statuses
- On-Time: 11.1%
- Early: 10.9%
- Late: 10.7%

⭐ Although margins appear similar, the operational risks associated with late deliveries make them less sustainable.

## Conclusion

Analysis of 180K+ supply chain records revealed that over 57% of orders were delivered late, with Standard Class shipments contributing nearly 60% of high-risk orders. Western Europe and Central America emerged as critical revenue regions, making logistics disruptions in these areas particularly impactful. The dashboard enables proactive monitoring of delivery performance, inventory risk, and regional exposure, helping operations teams prioritize interventions and improve supply chain efficiency.
